1 · Position + AI Overview Stack

0.07–0.13%

These answer the user's question instantly — no click needed. The 275K impressions are real, but clicks are stolen before they reach your listing. 9,079 impressions on the HVAC LSA page yielded only 6 clicks.

2 · Paid Competitor Ads Above Organic

4–6 ads

For high-intent queries like "LSA management for plumbers" and "digital marketing for home services," agencies like Cardinal Digital, Scorpion, and Lendio are running Google Ads that appear in positions 1–4 above all organic results. Users searching with purchase intent click the first compelling ad — not position #2 organic.

3 · Conversion Tracking Not Measuring Real Leads

322 "convs"

GA4 reports 322 total conversions (216 CPC, 95 Direct, 11 Organic) — but these are almost certainly micro-events (button clicks, page visits, chat widget opens), NOT actual contact form submissions or booked calls. If real lead forms were converting at this rate, you'd know about it. Conversion events need to be audited and tied to actual form submissions.

4 · Brand-Heavy Traffic Profile

35% brand

The top 4 keywords by clicks are all brand or near-brand ("real time marketing", "realtime marketing", "realtimemarketing", "real time marketing reviews"). People searching these terms already know you — they're not new leads. True top-of-funnel keywords ("LSA agency", "home service marketing company") generate almost zero clicks.

5 · Internal Traffic Polluting GA4

~51% noise

Of 17,209 GA4 sessions, approximately 8,820 come from internal sources: dashboard.realtimemarketing.com (3,220), client site referrals (goldstar: 236, inhouseplumbing: 114, canyonhills: 77, farmingtonvalley: 55, beisplumbing: 66), admin pages (/login, /reports, /user), and review widgets. 6 · Mobile Experience Gap

37.8% eng

Mobile accounts for 43.6% of GA4 sessions but only 37.8% engagement — the lowest of any device. With 91 of 364 organic clicks landing on mobile (25%), a poor mobile experience means leads are bouncing before they see a contact form. For B2B agencies, desktop converts better, but mobile can't be ignored when it's nearly half of traffic.

🔬 Conversion Tracking Audit — What GA4 Is Measuring vs What It Should Be

❌ What GA4 Is Likely Tracking (Current)

  • Clicks on "Get a Free Quote" or "Book a Call" CTA buttons (not form completions)
  • Scroll depth events (user scrolled to 50% or 75% of page)
  • Chat widget opens or chat session starts via embedded Unify360 widget
  • Page views of /contact/ treated as conversion (viewing ≠ submitting)
  • Time-on-page thresholds (30+ seconds = "engaged user" fired as conversion)
  • Dashboard login events from existing clients counted as conversions

✅ What GA4 Should Be Tracking (Real Leads)

  • Confirmed contact form submission — fire on /thank-you/ page view OR form_submit event
  • Inbound phone call tracked via CallRail or Google Ads call extension with GA4 import
  • Calendly / booking confirmation page view after a demo is actually scheduled
  • Newsletter opt-in form completed (distinct from marketing lead)
  • Chat conversation qualified as lead (via Unify360 CRM tag, imported to GA4)
  • Paid search conversions imported from Google Ads (not estimated)
Fix It in 4 Steps
1

Create a /thank-you/ redirect page for the contact form and track its pageview as the GA4 conversion event

2

Remove scroll depth and button click events from the Conversions list in GA4 Admin → Events

3

Connect CallRail (or Google Ads call tracking) to GA4 to import real phone lead conversions

4

Add GA4 internal traffic filter for Philippines IPs and dashboard.realtimemarketing.com referral
